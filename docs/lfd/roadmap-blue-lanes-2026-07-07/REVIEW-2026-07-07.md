# Review: blue-lane LFD handoffs (2026-07-07)

Reviewer: LFD-design pass over commit `c6ea7a260` (19 lanes + COMMON.md +
README.md), cross-checked against the live repo and the shared LFD harness
built in-tree under `lfd/_shared/` + `tests/integration/lfd/`.

**Verdict: keep the lanes — they are strong on target, constraints, and
entropy — but do not launch any lane as written.** The portfolio is missing
its fourth part: independently-owned instruments. As committed, each
implementation agent authors its own scorer, its own eval answers, and its
own blinding — the optimizer builds its own loss function, which un-blinds
every defense COMMON.md correctly specifies. The extension layer
(INSTRUMENTS.md + LANE-ADDENDA.md, this directory) closes this without
rewriting the lanes.

## What is strong (keep as-is)

- Lane split and product scoping are better-informed than repo-only
  derivation: Notion supplied the must-have bundle (lane 02), Slack
  "Model 1" scope (03), the Crisp-style secrets journey (04), the
  memory-platform pillar split (15–19), and NL permission management (08).
- COMMON.md carries the right shared physics: holdout-only acceptance,
  VOID semantics, capped dev feedback, capacity caps, both-direction
  metrics, stall/exploration entropy rules, security stop conditions.
- Per-lane cheat audits are specific and mostly instrumentable; several
  lanes (01, 04, 06, 09, 11, 19) demand known-good/known-bad scorer
  calibration — the right instinct.
- Path accuracy is high: of ~20 concrete repo paths cited, only 2 are
  wrong (see finding 4).

## Findings, by severity

### 1. BLOCKER — the optimizer authors its own loss function

COMMON.md "Required launch artifacts": *"the assigned agent must create …
`harness/score.sh` … `eval/dev/` … `eval/holdout/`"*. That makes every
downstream control self-referential:

- "Holdout answers outside every optimizer-readable mount" is impossible
  when the optimizer writes those answers first — it has seen them, and it
  chose them. Probe gap, blinding, and the holdout bar stop measuring
  anything.
- "score.sh must checksum the harness" — the same agent writes the
  checksummer and the checksummed. Cheat-museum exhibit 4 (scorer editing)
  is fenced by policy but not by mechanism.
- The agent can (and, under budget pressure, will) write contracts its
  planned implementation trivially satisfies. Acceptance becomes
  self-graded.

**Fix (INSTRUMENTS.md):** split roles. The designer session owns
`harness/`, eval answers, holdout placement, canaries, pins, and lint; the
implementation agent gets read-only instruments, one writable runner
profile, visible eval inputs, and LOG.md. The shared harness already
exists and is verified: scorer core (10/10 self-test incl. VOID,
probe-map blinding, holdout budget) and a data-driven Rust eval runner over
`RebornIntegrationHarness`.

### 2. MAJOR — instruments are prose; 19 hand-rolled scorers implied

No lane ships executable scoring. 19 independently-written `score.sh`
implementations means inconsistent VOID/feedback semantics, no shared leak
audit, and ~19× duplicated effort inside implementation budgets that are
already tight (finding 3). **Fix:** all lanes bind to
`lfd/_shared/scorer/` + `tests/integration/lfd/` (SCHEMA.md is the single
contract); per-lane deltas reduce to eval data + contracts + caps.json +
one runner profile.

### 3. MAJOR — eval sizes are unfundable inside the stated budgets

Lane 17 wants 120 dev + 300 holdout labeled traces in a 14 h lane that
also implements the feature; lane 19 wants 220 dev + 560 holdout
meta-cases in 14 h. Combined with finding 1 (the implementer would author
them), the realistic outcome is fabricated or template-stamped evals.
**Fix:** designer-authored initial sets (30–60 dev / 10–15 holdout per
lane, contract-style), with the lane's larger numbers reframed as a
growth target owned by the designer/patch role, never the implementer.
The small-eval enumerability warning stays mandatory in every lane.

### 4. MINOR — two phantom paths

- `crates/ironclaw_webui_v2_static` (lanes 01, 05) does not exist →
  `crates/ironclaw_webui_v2/static/` + `crates/ironclaw_webui_v2/frontend/`.
- `docs/reborn/2026-06-22-memory-as-product-layer.md` (lane 15) does not
  exist in the tree or git history → nearest real sources:
  `docs/plans/2026-06-23-hermes-style-context-management.md`,
  `contracts/memory.md`, `contracts/memory-profiles.md`. Lane 15 already
  hedges correctly ("record the missing source").

Everything else cited checks out, including `scripts/reborn_qa_matrix`,
`FEATURE_PARITY.md`, `crates/ironclaw_host_runtime/src/egress/credential.rs`,
`tests/integration/secret_injection.rs`, `crates/ironclaw_llm/src/transcription/`
(an OpenAI-shaped STT seam already exists — lane 10 is right to build on
it), and `crates/ironclaw_product_context`.

### 5. MINOR — no dependency or conflict map

Handing all 19 lanes to concurrent agents as README suggests ("one lane
directory per implementation agent") guarantees collisions: {03,05} share
Slack composition; {04,09} share host_runtime credential mediation;
{07,08,12} share authorization/approvals; {13,14,15,16,17,18,19} all sit
on the memory family; 01/02 are rollups over other lanes' surfaces.
**Fix:** LANE-ADDENDA.md adds launch waves and a conflict table; umbrella
lanes 01/15 (and partially 02/14) are recast as meta-scorer rollups over
their pillar lanes' holdouts rather than independent code-editing loops.

### 6. MINOR — enforcement-parameter drift vs COMMON.md

COMMON.md says dev feedback ≤ 5 misses and holdout ≤ 3 calls / 24 h. The
shared scorer as first built printed ≤ 10 worst cases and enforced a 12 h
window (which permits 6/24 h — weaker). **Fix applied to the scorer, not
the docs:** cap = 5, window = 24 h (see INSTRUMENTS.md; scorer self-test
re-run after the change).

### 7. NOTE — scope deltas vs the in-tree design briefs

`lfd/_briefs/` (12 designer briefs written this session from repo evidence)
and the lanes disagree in informative ways; LANE-ADDENDA.md maps each lane
to its brief and reconciles: lane 08 adds an NL-command layer on top of the
brief's approval-substrate parity; lane 13 reframes self-learning as
memory-write/retrieval pillars where the brief centered skill distillation
(both are kept: the brief's skill-distillation themes become part of lane
13's eval); lane 11's mission state machine supersedes the brief's
meta-prompt focus; lane 14 ≈ brief long-term-memory but acceptance-framed
over lanes 15–18. The two phantom-path corrections above flow back into
the briefs (voice brief wrongly claimed "no STT seam exists").

## Launch preconditions (summary)

A lane may launch when: (1) its eval (inputs + sealed contracts + off-repo
holdout) exists and was authored by the designer role; (2) its runner
profile skeleton compiles; (3) `score.sh` produced a real number and a
deliberate canary trip produced exactly `VOID: constraint violation`;
(4) pins.json is filled; (5) the lane's ADDENDA entry (paths, Stage-0
commands, wave) is applied. INSTRUMENTS.md defines the mechanics; the
in-tree harness supplies them.
